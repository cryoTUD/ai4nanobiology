__Course description in SQUILL:__  

This course introduces students to the fundamental principles and practical applications of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning methods (ML) in the context of nanobiology and biophysics. 
The course covers key methods such as deep learning, supervised and unsupervised learning, and their applications to protein structure prediction, bioimage analysis, and biological data interpretation. 
Students will explore the capabilities and limitations of AI tools, address ethical considerations in AI-driven biological research, and gain hands-on experience through computational practicals. 
The course consists of lectures, coursework, and guided programming assignments.

_Contact hours (24), Homework (20 hours), Group project & poster presentation (40 hours)_

__Learning objectives__  

By the end of the course, students will be able to:   

- Understand key AI/ML concepts relevant to biological data analysis, including supervised and unsupervised learning, neural networks, generative models and model evaluation

- Apply AI methods to real-world problems in nanobiology, such as protein structure prediction and microscopy image analysis

- Critically assess the capabilities and limitations of AI tools in the biological sciences.

- Implement and interpret computational models using Python-based tools

- Critically reflect on ethical and societal implications of AI in biological research

- Collaborate in problem-solving tasks that integrate computational and biological knowledge

__Course activities__  

Combined lectures/computer practicals, self-study, group project, poster presentation
